The New SEO: 4 Easy Ways to Win in AI Search

Published October 4, 2024 | By Nicole Jolie

The Game Has Changed: AI Search ≠ Google Search

AI search engines like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini do not display a list of links. They generate answers. Instead of showing who has the best keywords, they show who has the best information.

If you want your firm, product, or expertise to appear in those AI-generated answers, you need to optimize differently.

That is where Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) comes in.

Here are four simple, proven tactics to help you win in the new era of AI search.

1. Be the Answer, Not Just Another Link

AI search engines do not crawl for links. They pull clarity. They want straightforward, factual statements that explain why your firm or product is worth recommending.

How to do it:

  • Create pages that directly answer key questions about your field.
  • Use tables, comparisons, pros/cons lists, and short summaries to make your content scannable.
  • Add short credibility cues like: "Best value for startups" or "Trusted by inventors since 1999."
  • Write content in plain language without jargon or unnecessary complexity.

Why it works:

When you make your information easy for AI to extract and understand, your expertise becomes the answer it selects.

2. Make Your Website Easy for Robots (and Humans)

AI engines rely on what they can see and read clearly. If your site is overloaded with design fluff or missing structured data, you are invisible to both bots and clients.

How to do it:

  • Use bullet points and tables to highlight facts like pricing, reviews, and case results.
  • Keep your layout clean and distraction-free.
  • Use Schema markup (but make sure it matches the actual content users can see).
  • Avoid pop-ups or ad-heavy layouts that block main content.

Why it works:

AI search prioritizes clarity and structure. The simpler your pages, the more likely you will be indexed and recommended.

3. Build Trust Through Third-Party Mentions

AI engines do not take your word for it. They look for validation from reputable sources. Mentions in credible directories, roundups, or reviews act as trust signals.

How to do it:

  • Get featured in respected directories such as Chambers & Partners or Top 10 Legal Experts lists.
  • Participate in industry roundups or expert panels.
  • Encourage clients and peers to leave verified reviews on Google and niche platforms.
  • Use public relations strategically. Being mentioned once on a high-authority site can outperform dozens of blog posts.

Why it works:

The more independent, trustworthy sources confirm your expertise, the more confidently AI will recommend you.

4. Create Content That Follows the Client Journey

AI does not just answer "what to buy." It guides users from "what to choose" to "how to use it." Your content should cover that entire path.

How to do it:

  • Write content for every stage of the decision process:
    • Awareness: "How to choose an IP attorney"
    • Consideration: "Top 5 mistakes startups make with trademarks"
    • After purchase: "How to protect your patent internationally"
  • Add detailed FAQs and troubleshooting guides.
  • Create content that genuinely helps, not just sells.

Why it works:

AI favors complete, context-rich answers. The more comprehensive your coverage, the more likely you will show up at multiple points in a client's journey.

The Bottom Line

Winning in AI search is not about keyword stuffing. It is about clarity, credibility, and completeness.

Here is your quick checklist to dominate:

  • Provide clear, factual answers without fluff.
  • Format your content so AI can easily read it.
  • Build authority through trusted, third-party mentions.
  • Cover every stage of the customer journey.

Follow these four GEO tactics, and your business will become the go-to answer instead of just another search result.
