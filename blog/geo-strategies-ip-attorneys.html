GEO Strategies for IP Attorneys: Make Your Firm Easy to Find in AI Search

Published October 4, 2024 | By Nicole Jolie

Why IP Attorneys Need to Pay Attention to AI Search

AI search engines like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini are becoming the first stop for people seeking answers about intellectual property. Unlike traditional search engines, AI doesn't just list websites. It generates answers using trusted sources across the web.

If your firm isn't showing up there, potential clients might never find you. Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) is the solution. It helps your firm appear in AI-generated answers by emphasizing credibility, visibility, and expertise.

Here are four practical GEO strategies every IP attorney can apply today.

1. Fix Citation Gaps: Show What Makes Your Firm Stand Out

AI looks for trusted sources, not just your website. If competitors are mentioned in law reviews, blogs, or legal guides but your firm is not, AI may overlook you.

Action Steps:

  • Identify key IP publications where you're noted, quoted, or published including blogs and legal journals where thought leadership is valued.
  • Share your expertise through articles, guest posts, or interviews, especially podcasts that highlight wins in patent litigation, trademark defense, or trade secret protection. Audio files are an excellent way to establish authority (think: SoundCloud, YouTube, Facebook, etc).
  • Get cited in respected publications and send in your case findings to those publications. Even a few mentions can significantly increase your visibility. Make sure you define the call to action and exactly what you want from that citation to the publication so that they're publishing so that you get the credit and people who find this citation can reach out to you.

Why it matters:

These citations act as endorsements, authority, and credibility that AI can rely on, putting your firm in front of potential clients who trust authoritative sources.

2. Join the Right Conversations: Demonstrate an Integrated Approach

AI search engines also scan real-world conversations, from LinkedIn posts to forum discussions. Demonstrating how your firm integrates prosecution and litigation builds credibility in the eyes of AI and humans.

Action Steps:

  • Engage in IP law communities on LinkedIn, Reddit, and professional forums.
  • Share anonymized case stories showing your end-to-end support for clients, from filing patents to defending them in court.
  • Offer practical advice, not promotional content, to establish authority.

Why it matters:

AI and potential clients value authentic engagement. By joining these conversations, your firm becomes a trusted voice in your field.

3. Publish on High-Value IP Topics: Highlight Confidentiality

AI prefers current, detailed, and practical content. Writing about high-value IP topics and demonstrating strict confidentiality practices shows that your firm is both knowledgeable and trustworthy.

Action Steps:

  • Create articles on timely IP topics: trade secret protection, global patent strategies, or litigation trends.
  • Include anonymized client examples to illustrate your methods for safeguarding confidential information.
  • Build resource pages, such as "Prosecution vs. Litigation in Pharma IP," and update them regularly.
  • Highlight professional memberships (INTA, AIPLA, local bar associations and even where you spoke last at any business events, including Chamber of Commerce) to reinforce credibility.

Why it matters:

Clients and AI both reward firms that demonstrate expertise and maintain strict confidentiality. It positions your firm as a reliable authority.

4. Be Visible and Trusted

When you combine citation coverage, meaningful engagement, authoritative content, and clear confidentiality practices, your IP firm will rise in AI search rankings.

This goes beyond technical SEO like schema. It's about real-world visibility and trust. When clients see your firm cited, mentioned in discussions, and consistently producing useful insights, AI recognizes your authority and so do potential clients.

Bottom Line:

Being visible and trusted in AI search is not optional. It's the new way to attract high-value IP clients before they even search for your website.
