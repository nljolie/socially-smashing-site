About Nicole Jolie

From $100 in My Pocket to Building a 7-Figure AI Authority Business

I moved to New York City on January 3rd 1985 with $100, no high school diploma, no job, and a dream of making it on Wall Street. It was the middle of winter and there was a blizzard. I never lived in snow. I was 17 years old, alone, no friends or family and had nothing but determination.

Within months I had my Series 7 license and was working on the floor of the New York Stock Exchange as a runner. Over the next 11 years, I worked for Goldman Sachs, Bear Stearns, Donaldson Lufkin Jenrette, and Shearson Lehman. I then worked at NBC scheduling satellite trucks for feeds and as a part time grip on shows such as Saturday Night Live before transitioning to selling legal advertisements in The National Law Journal.

I learned how money moves, how markets think, and how to survive in environments where one mistake costs you everything.

I went on to study Finance at San Jose State University and Political Science at Fordham University in New York City. But my real education came from building businesses, failing, learning, and doing it again.

Building Businesses Across Industries (Before Most People Had Email)

In 2005, I became a USAT-certified triathlon coach and NASM-certified personal trainer. I coached over 80 athletes to their first triathlons and age division wins, icluding Napoleon McCallum of the Oakland Raiders. I learned how to take someone from I can't do this to crossing finish lines they never thought possible.

That's when I realized, coaching businesses isn't that different from coaching athletes. It's about systems, endurance, and showing up when it's hard.

In 2008, I started learning Google Ads. Then Facebook Ads when most businesses were still figuring out what Facebook even was. I grew my own page to over 10,000 followers in less than a month using penny ads and taught others how to do the same.

I've built over 100 websites. I launched my own skincare line, Radiance Naturally Advanced SkinCare, and sold it on Amazon and Shopify. I worked with MLM coaches making 7 figures a year. I've worked with best-selling authors, top coaches, and business owners who needed someone who understood marketing, systems, and ROI.

My career has taken me across seven countries. I've been a solopreneur since before it was a buzzword. I've made money, lost money, rebuilt, and scaled. I've done this for almost 40 years, and I'm still here because I adapt faster than most people realize the game has changed.

Then AI Changed Everything (And I Was Ready)

In late 2022, when ChatGPT launched, I didn't panic. I got curious.

While other consultants were trying to figure out what AI even meant for their business, I was building GPTs, designing automations with Make and Zapier, and studying how AI systems think, learn, and make recommendations.

Early in my AI journey, I worked on the Knowledge Business Blueprint chatbot with Tony Robbins and Dean Graziosi's development team. That taught me how to build systems that work under pressure, not just in demos.

But here's what really set me apart, I did the research nobody else was doing.

While everyone was selling AI marketing strategies and promising magic results, I spent hundreds of hours analyzing over 250 healthcare businesses to understand why some show up in AI search and most don't.

What I found shocked me, 89% of healthcare practices are completely invisible in AI search, despite having beautiful websites, great reviews, and decades of experience.

That research became my edge. It's why I can tell you exactly where you rank in ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI, and Gemini. And more importantly, why you're not showing up where you should be.
